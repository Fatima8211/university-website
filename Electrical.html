BS Electrical Engineering Program

The department offers a fully accredited BS Electrical Engineering program and HEC approved.

Power Systems

Build a strong foundation in power generation, transmission, distribution, and protection systems.

Electronics

Explore electronic circuits, semiconductor devices, and modern communication systems.

Control Systems

Address contemporary challenges in automation, robotics, and smart grid technologies.

Program Educational Objectives (PEOs)

The faculty has defined the educational objectives of BS Electrical Engineering Program with input from our affiliates and alumni.

PEO 1: Technical Proficiency

Building on fundamental knowledge, graduates should continue to develop professionally and ethically within and across disciplines.

PEO 2: Communication & Management

Will be endowed with effective oral and written communication skills to lead teams in multi-disciplinary environments.

PEO 3: Global Society

Contribute to society in electrical engineering with an understanding of contemporary issues for the betterment of society.

Program Learning Outcomes (PLOs)

i.

Engineering Knowledge

Apply mathematics, science, and engineering fundamentals to solve complex problems

ii.

Problem Analysis

Identify and analyze complex engineering problems using first principles

iii.

Design Solutions

Design solutions that meet specified needs with consideration for public health and safety

iv.

Investigation

Investigate complex problems through systematic experimentation and analysis

v.

Modern Tool Usage

Select and apply appropriate techniques and modern engineering tools

vi.

The Engineer & Society

Apply reasoning to assess societal, health, safety, and cultural issues

vii.

Environment & Sustainability

Understand the impact of engineering solutions on society and the environment

viii.

Ethics

Understand the impact of professional engineering solutions in societal contexts

ix.

Individual & Team Work

Work effectively as an individual or in multidisciplinary teams

x.

Project Management

Demonstrate management skills and apply engineering principles to manage projects

Our Facilities

State-of-the-art laboratories for hands-on learning and research

Electric Machines Lab

Electric Machines & Power Electronics Laboratory

Step into a realm where electrical engineering meets innovation. Our laboratory is a hub of hands-on learning and research excellence. Students delve into the inner workings of induction motors, synchronous machines, transformers, and DC machines, gaining invaluable insights into their operation, control, and real-world applications.

Under the guidance of our experienced faculty and technicians, students embark on a journey of experiential learning, innovation, and discovery.

Smart Grid Lab

Power Systems & Smart Grid Laboratory

The Power Systems and Smart Grid Laboratory (PSSG Lab) is a beacon of cutting-edge innovation. Equipped with top-tier modules from renowned manufacturers like Lucas Nulle and Opal-RT OP5700, this lab serves as a dynamic hub for research and education.

This multi-functional facility is where theory transforms into practical understanding, fostering the development of future leaders in the field of electrical engineering and smart grid innovation.
